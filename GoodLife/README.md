# GoodLife
> Institutional Health Management Platform

GoodLife is a comprehensive, cloud-native health management platform designed for healthcare institutions operating across multiple branches, cities, and countries. It unifies the workflows of patients, clinicians, administrative staff, and third-party stakeholders into a single, secure, role-driven system — accessible via web and mobile interfaces from anywhere in the world.

---

## Vision

Healthcare institutions today operate across fragmented tools — separate systems for scheduling, records, billing, and communication. GoodLife replaces that fragmentation with one coherent platform, purpose-built for the real operational complexity of modern healthcare: multi-branch coordination, insurance workflows, regulatory compliance, and patient-centered care — all from a single login.

---

## Who Uses GoodLife

### Patients
Patients have a secure personal portal where they can:
- Register and manage their profile and medical history
- Book, reschedule, or cancel appointments at any branch
- View visit summaries, diagnoses, prescriptions, and lab results
- Track ongoing treatment plans and medication schedules
- Submit and track insurance claims
- Receive appointment reminders, medication alerts, and follow-up notifications
- Securely message their care team
- Monitor vitals over time (weight, blood pressure, glucose, etc.)
- Download personal health documents (discharge summaries, referral letters, test results)

### Doctors & Health Practitioners
Clinicians are the operational core of the platform:
- View and manage their full patient roster across branches
- Access complete patient health records — history, diagnoses, prescriptions, test results
- Schedule and manage appointments; view daily/weekly/monthly calendars
- Record consultation notes, diagnoses, and treatment plans during or after visits
- Issue prescriptions digitally and update medication records
- Order lab tests and receive results directly in the patient's record
- Send secure messages and notifications to patients
- Refer patients to specialists within or outside the institution
- Monitor tracked vitals and health data submitted by patients
- Collaborate with colleagues and co-manage shared patients

### Management & Administrators
Institutional administrators have oversight of all operational activity:
- Manage all staff accounts — onboarding, role assignments, branch assignments, deactivation
- Approve and verify new user registrations (patients, practitioners, third-party providers)
- Monitor system-wide activity, appointment volumes, and health outcomes across all branches
- Configure branch-specific settings, working hours, and available services
- Access audit logs for compliance, data access, and system activity
- Manage billing, invoicing, and payment records
- Generate and export operational and clinical reports
- Oversee insurance claim pipelines and third-party integrations
- Manage institution-wide announcements and internal communications

### Third-Party Providers (Insurance & Partners)
External stakeholders have a controlled, permissioned portal:
- Access relevant patient records with explicit consent and audit trails
- Submit, review, and update insurance claims tied to specific visits
- Download authorized medical documentation (discharge summaries, lab results, referral letters)
- Communicate with practitioners and administrators on specific cases
- Receive automated notifications on claim status changes and required documentation
- View approved benefit utilization summaries per patient

## Multi-Branch, Multi-Region Architecture

GoodLife is designed from the ground up to operate across an institution's entire footprint — whether that is two clinics in the same city or a network of hospitals across multiple countries.

- Each branch is a configurable entity with its own staff, schedules, services, and settings
- Patients can be seen across branches; their records follow them institution-wide
- Management can operate at branch level, regional level, or institution-wide level
- Timezone, language, and regulatory settings are configurable per region
- Data residency requirements are respected — data can be scoped to specific jurisdictions where required
